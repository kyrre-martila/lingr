# Lingr — README

A slow dating app built around presence, reflection and intentional connection.

---

# What is Lingr?

Lingr is a slow dating app designed for people who are tired of swipe culture.

Most dating apps optimize for:

- speed
- endless discovery
- instant judgment
- maximum engagement

Lingr attempts to do the opposite.

Instead of endless profiles and dopamine loops, Lingr focuses on a very small number of intentional connections.

People are revealed gradually through conversation, reflection and mutual investment.

The goal is not maximum screen time.

The goal is meaningful connection.

---

# Core Philosophy

Most dating apps encourage people to process each other quickly.

Lingr is built around a different belief:

> Some people are worth taking time with.

The app is intentionally designed to create:

- slower emotional pacing
- curiosity before judgment
- emotional safety
- intentional attention
- genuine presence

Not:

- addictive engagement loops
- endless swiping
- popularity systems
- gamified interaction

Lingr should feel closer to a thoughtful journal than a social media feed.

---

# Core Concepts

---

# Glimps

A Glimps is a unified emotional moment.

Not a selfie-first profile.
Not a polished personal brand.

A Glimps combines:

- photo or short video
- reflection text
- optional audio or music

Examples:

- rain on a window
- coffee at sunrise
- a quiet room
- a train ride
- candlelight
- a thought
- a memory

Glimps are designed to communicate atmosphere and inner life before appearance dominates perception.

---

# Spark

A Spark is an intentional expression of interest.

Unlike a swipe or traditional “like”:

- Sparks are limited
- Sparks are quiet
- Sparks are blind

The receiver sees:

> “Someone noticed you today.”

But they do not immediately see who.

Identity is intentionally delayed.

The goal is to create curiosity before instant appearance-based filtering.

---

# Connection

A Connection forms when two people Spark each other mutually.

Once connected:

- chat opens
- Layers begin unlocking
- deeper emotional context becomes available

Connections are designed to feel calm and spacious — not transactional.

---

# Layers

Lingr profiles reveal themselves gradually.

Not all at once.

Layers unlock through:

- conversation
- active days
- Pulse participation
- mutual investment

Not through payment.

Early layers focus on:

- energy
- atmosphere
- reflection
- emotional tone

Later layers reveal:

- clearer photos
- personal stories
- emotional history
- deeper compatibility

The system exists to reduce snap judgment and encourage emotional pacing.

---

# Pulse

Pulse is a daily reflective prompt system.

Examples:

> “What made you feel calm today?”

> “What has been on your mind lately?”

> “What felt meaningful today?”

Pulse is not a quiz.

It is meant to slowly accumulate emotional familiarity over time.

---

# Window

Window is a mutual deepening state.

Both users actively choose to enter it.

When Window opens:

- discovery slows down
- focus increases
- more Layers unlock
- the connection becomes more intentional

Window represents:

> “Let’s give this real attention.”

---

# Snuggle

Snuggle is a lightweight real-time co-presence feature.

Both users simply hold simultaneously.

No score.
No streaks.
No rewards.

Just shared presence.

---

# Business Model

Lingr does NOT lock emotional depth behind payment.

Core relationship features remain free.

Payment only affects discovery breadth.

Free users receive:

- 1 person per day

Paid users receive:

- 3–5 people per day

The philosophy is simple:

> Depth is always free.

---

# Design Philosophy

Lingr should feel:

- warm
- spacious
- quiet
- emotionally safe
- premium

The interface intentionally avoids:

- streaks
- badges
- “top picks”
- manipulative notifications
- endless scrolling
- casino-like engagement systems

The app is designed to slow interaction down slightly.

Not enough to frustrate people.

Just enough to help them actually notice each other.

---

# Privacy & Security

Lingr treats emotional and relational data as sensitive.

Core privacy rules include:

- no raw location exposure
- no permanent public media URLs
- no user IDs in discovery
- no client-trusted Layer logic
- signed expiring media URLs
- server-side permission enforcement

Identity is intentionally revealed gradually.

The safest information is often the information that is never exposed in the first place.
