# Lingr — User Journey

Defines the emotional and functional journey of a Lingr user from onboarding to deep connection.

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Lingr should feel over time.

Not just:

txt id="d2m5r7" what users do 

But:

txt id="p8v1k4" what users emotionally experience 

Every stage of the journey should reinforce Lingr’s core philosophy:

txt id="u7q3x9" some people are worth taking time with 

---

# Core Emotional Arc

Most dating apps create this emotional pattern:

txt id="m0k9v2" curiosity → overstimulation → comparison → exhaustion → detachment 

Lingr attempts to create:

txt id="c6n4t1" curiosity → emotional safety → intentional attention → gradual intimacy → meaningful connection 

---

# User Journey Overview

txt id="j5r8x0" 1. First Impression 2. Onboarding 3. First Glimps Creation 4. Daily Discovery 5. Receiving a Spark 6. Mutual Connection 7. Layer Progression 8. Window 9. Snuggle 10. Leaving the App 

---

# 1. First Impression

---

# Emotional Goal

The user should immediately feel:

txt id="v2m7k5" “This is calmer than other dating apps.” 

Not:

txt id="t4q1z8" “I need to optimize myself.” 

---

# Landing Experience

The opening experience should communicate:

txt id="o6r3m9" - softness - warmth - slowness - emotional maturity 

Avoid:

txt id="g8k0v6" - flashy onboarding - aggressive conversion tactics - high-energy animations 

---

# Intro Messaging

Core messaging examples:

txt id="f9p2x4" “Some people are worth taking time with.”  “Not endless swipes. Just a few people worth noticing.”  “Depth is always free.” 

Tone should feel:

txt id="u1w7n3" gentle and emotionally grounded 

---

# 2. Onboarding

---

# Emotional Goal

Users should feel:

txt id="n3m9q6" understood, not evaluated 

Onboarding is not:

txt id="r5x2v0" a personality test 

It is:

txt id="c8k4t7" a gradual emotional introduction 

---

# Onboarding Flow

Suggested sequence:

txt id="x7m5u1" 1. Values 2. Recharge styles 3. Connection intention 4. Reflection prompt 5. Create Glimps 6. Presence promise 

---

# Values Step

Purpose:

txt id="b0r8v3" understand emotional orientation 

Examples:

txt id="e6k1p7" - Calmness - Creativity - Humor - Stability - Curiosity 

The UI should feel exploratory.

Not diagnostic.

---

# Recharge Styles

Purpose:

txt id="q4m7n2" understand pacing compatibility 

Examples:

txt id="t1v5k9" - reading - nature - social evenings - music - quiet mornings 

Reason:

txt id="j8p0x4" people often connect through emotional tempo 

---

# Reflection Prompt

Example:

txt id="v5q3m1" “What have you been thinking about lately?” 

Purpose:

txt id="d9r6k8" encourage emotional openness early 

Avoid:

txt id="u0n2v7" performative bios 

---

# 3. First Glimps Creation

---

# Emotional Goal

Users should feel:

txt id="m7k4x2" invited to share atmosphere, not perform identity 

---

# What a Glimps Is

A Glimps is:

txt id="h3v9r1" a unified emotional moment 

It combines:

txt id="o8m5t6" - image or video - reflection text - optional audio 

---

# Important UX Rule

The app should explicitly communicate:

txt id="x6r1k4" Glimps are not primarily selfies. 

Encourage:

txt id="z2v8m7" - places - moods - moments - textures - environments 

Examples:

txt id="y4k0n9" - coffee in rain - train windows - forests - books - candlelight 

---

# Audio Philosophy

Audio should feel:

txt id="c5m3x8" personal and atmospheric 

Not:

txt id="f1r7v2" performative voice-note culture 

---

# 4. Daily Discovery

---

# Emotional Goal

Users should feel:

txt id="n8k6p4" calm curiosity 

Not:

txt id="t3m9v0" compulsive urgency 

---

# Discovery Experience

Users receive:

txt id="p5x1r7" 1–5 intentional people per day 

Not endless feed scrolling.

---

# Discovery Cards

Discovery cards emphasize:

txt id="m2v8q1" - atmosphere - emotional energy - reflection - pacing 

Identity remains partially hidden.

---

# Key Psychological Effect

The system should create:

txt id="j4n7x6" dwell time 

Users should naturally pause longer.

---

# 5. Sending a Spark

---

# Emotional Goal

A Spark should feel:

txt id="g0m4v8" intentional and vulnerable 

Not casual.

---

# Spark Philosophy

A Spark is:

txt id="w7r2k1" a quiet expression of attention 

Not:

txt id="d8v5m3" a swipe 

---

# Limited Sparks

Limited Sparks intentionally create:

txt id="k6x0p9" emotional weight 

Users should think:

txt id="r3n8v2" “Do I actually want to notice this person?” 

---

# Receiving a Spark

Receiver sees:

txt id="f5v1q7" someone noticed them 

But not identity yet.

This creates:

txt id="u9m4x6" curiosity before appearance fixation 

---

# 6. Mutual Connection

---

# Emotional Goal

Connection should feel:

txt id="t8k2m5" quietly meaningful 

Not explosive.

---

# Match Moment

Avoid:

txt id="d4v7p1" confetti celebration sounds aggressive animations 

Instead:

txt id="m1x8q3" soft reveal subtle transition gentle emotional shift 

---

# First Connection State

At Layer 1 users gain access to:

txt id="r7m5v0" - display name - approximate age range - city - blurred avatar - values - Glimps 

The first chat should feel:

txt id="p9k3x4" hopeful but calm 

---

# Opening Prompt

The system may provide gentle prompts:

Examples:

txt id="q6v2m8" “What’s been the best part of your week so far?”  “What kind of quiet do you enjoy most?” 

Prompts should encourage:

txt id="y0r5k7" real conversation 

Not:

txt id="n4v8m1" forced icebreakers 

---

# 7. Layer Progression

---

# Emotional Goal

Users should gradually feel:

txt id="j1m7x5" “I’m beginning to understand this person.” 

---

# Layer Experience

Each Layer unlock should feel:

txt id="r8v4p2" earned through presence 

Not:

txt id="w3m0k6" gamified progression 

---

# Psychological Effect

The gradual reveal system exists to reduce:

txt id="x5r9v1" - snap judgment - disposable behavior - premature overexposure 

---

# Pulse Integration

Pulse answers slowly accumulate emotional context.

By Layer 3 users begin seeing:

txt id="q2k7m4" shared emotional history 

Not merely profile facts.

---

# 8. Window

---

# Emotional Goal

Window should feel:

txt id="u5v8x3" intentional and focused 

It represents:

txt id="c1m4r9" mutual emotional investment 

---

# Entering Window

Both users must actively choose:

txt id="o7k2v5" to give this connection more attention 

This should psychologically feel different from normal matching.

---

# UX Changes During Window

Potential effects:

txt id="y8m1q4" - calmer interface - softer visual tone - reduced discovery emphasis - more emotional focus 

---

# Window Philosophy

Window says:

txt id="g4v9r2" “Let’s slow down and see what this becomes.” 

---

# 9. Snuggle

---

# Emotional Goal

Snuggle should feel:

txt id="p3m7x8" small, quiet and emotionally intimate 

---

# Snuggle Interaction

Two people:

txt id="v1r5k9" hold simultaneously 

That’s all.

No score.

No progression.

No rewards.

---

# Why Snuggle Exists

Purpose:

txt id="u6m2q4" co-presence without performance 

A tiny shared moment.

---

# 10. Leaving the App

---

# Emotional Goal

Lingr should ultimately encourage:

txt id="k7v3m1" people leaving the platform together 

Not staying indefinitely.

---

# Success Definition

Success is NOT:

txt id="j2m8x5" maximum retention 

Success is:

txt id="f4r0v7" meaningful human connection 

---

# Healthy Exit Philosophy

The app should emotionally support:

txt id="x8m5q2" natural departure 

Not dependency.

---

# Anti-Addiction Philosophy

Lingr intentionally avoids:

txt id="o3k7v6" - infinite feeds - streaks - urgency loops - engagement manipulation 

Reason:

txt id="y1m4r9" healthy emotional connection requires spaciousness 

---

# Long-Term Emotional Goal

Users should remember Lingr as:

txt id="u0v8m3" a calm place where people actually noticed each other 

Not:

txt id="n6k2x7" another exhausting dating app 

---

# Final Philosophy

The entire user journey should reinforce one emotional truth:

txt id="r4m9v1" real connection usually grows slowly 

Lingr exists to protect that slowness.
